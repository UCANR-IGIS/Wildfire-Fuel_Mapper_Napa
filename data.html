Step 3: Know Your Topography

The map shows streams, riparian area, and steep slopes greater than 30%. Riparian areas along streams and creeks are relatively fire resilient and not recommended for treatment within 20 feet of banks because of regulations that prevent vegetation removal. Steep slopes are hard to access and very costly to treat.



Step 4: Know Your Fuels

Different vegetation types burn at different rates and intensities. This map shows the different types of fuel on your parcel. Different fuels require different approaches for treatment. The table below shows the acres of each fuel type and the appropriate treatment options.

Step 5: Plan Your Fuel Treatments

The map includes the area near structures and roads that should be considered first when planning fuels treatments. Fuel treatments to protect life and property are most impactful to create defensible space 100 feet from structures. Treating 50 feet from roads is important for safe evacuation during wildfire.


Use the drop downs below to change the distances treated from roads and structures. The map and the table below will update accordingly. The table summarizes the number of acres of each fuel type and provides a range of potential treatment costs. Costs are based on a typical project; your actual costs may differ.

Step 6: Generate A Fuel Treatment Report

Based on your planned treatment areas (Step 5), you can create a PDF report summarizing available datasets, including fuel types, associated treatment costs, vegetation, topography, and additional environmental and jurisdictional information. This report will have much of the information needed to apply for fuels reduction grants.


Click the button to generate your PDF report. Make sure to allow pop-ups in your browser.
